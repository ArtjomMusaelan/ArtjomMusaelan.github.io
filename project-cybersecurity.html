Project 05

Cybersecurity Policy Research for Small Hotels

Research Analyst r=0.456 Correlation GDPR Compliant Team of 5 Mixed Methods Policy Recommendations
Overview

Evidence-based cybersecurity policy for hospitality SMEs

Led a 5-person research team conducting comprehensive academic investigation of employee cybersecurity awareness impact on security posture of small hotels, addressing critical gap between knowledge and practice in resource-constrained hospitality enterprises.

As Team Lead, coordinated mixed-methods research approach combining quantitative surveys and qualitative structured interviews, completed in partnership with DigiWerkplaats Breda over 8-week period (September-November 2024).

The Challenge

Bridging the cybersecurity knowledge-practice gap

Small hotels face significant cybersecurity challenges due to limited resources, lack of dedicated IT staff, and gaps in employee awareness. Despite increasing cyber threats targeting hospitality industry, little empirical research existed on effective training approaches.

The challenge was identifying which factors most effectively improve cybersecurity practices while being realistic for resource-constrained organizations, requiring rigorous research methodology with full GDPR compliance and ethics board approval.

Research Methodology

Team leadership in mixed-methods research design

Quantitative Research Component

  • — Designed and distributed online surveys via Qualtrics measuring age, confidence levels, training frequency, and perceived impact
  • — Processed data using Python with scikit-learn in Jupyter Notebooks for statistical correlation analysis
  • — Conducted demographic evaluation across hotel employee sample
  • — Applied statistical testing to identify significant relationships between variables

Qualitative Research Component

  • — Conducted structured interviews (in-person and via Microsoft Teams) with hotel employees
  • — Recorded audio (MP3 format) under informed consent with AI-powered transcription
  • — Performed manual verification of transcriptions ensuring accuracy
  • — Applied systematic thematic coding identifying 9 major themes across 5 interview transcripts

Ethics & Compliance Framework

  • — BUas Ethics Board: Approved as Medium Risk category with full compliance review
  • — GDPR Compliance: Encrypted storage, restricted access with 2FA, complete anonymization
  • — Data Retention: 10-year institutional policy with secure institutional systems
  • — Informed Consent: Voluntary participation with withdrawal rights, participant data access
  • — Research Integrity: Netherlands Code of Conduct (2018) and FAIR principles
  • — Documentation: Complete data management with codebook and privacy protocols

Research Findings & Impact

Evidence-based recommendations for effective training programs

Key insights challenging common assumptions while providing actionable guidance for resource-constrained hospitality organizations.

r=0.456

Training Frequency Correlation

Moderate positive correlation between training frequency and security protocol adherence, with optimal results from quarterly or biannual interactive sessions.

3-6 Mo.

Optimal Training Schedule

Regular training every 3-6 months balances engagement and retention without causing training fatigue, maintaining knowledge effectiveness.

9 Themes

Qualitative Insights

Systematic thematic analysis across 5 interviews revealed 9 major themes including role-specific needs and leadership importance.

No Age Gap

Universal Training Approach

No significant difference in cybersecurity awareness across age groups, supporting universal programs without age-based customization.

Policy Recommendations Delivered

  • — Regular Training Cycles: Implement 3-6 month intervals maintaining knowledge retention
  • — Interactive Approach: Scenario-based exercises tailored to specific roles (front desk, management)
  • — Security Culture: Visible leadership support with regular reminders integrated into workflows
  • — Universal Programs: Foundational knowledge for all employees regardless of demographics
  • — Third-Party Support: Consider external training providers addressing resource limitations
  • — Future Research: Identified 7 areas for continued investigation including longitudinal studies
